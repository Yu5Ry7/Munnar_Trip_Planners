Top station

when traveling from Munnar to Top Station, there are several tourist spots along the way that you can visit. Here are some popular attractions: Mattupetty Dam: Located around 13 kilometers from Munnar, Mattupetty Dam offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and a beautiful reservoir. You can enjoy boating and also visit the nearby dairy farm. Echo Point: Around 15 kilometers from Munnar, Echo Point is known for its natural echo phenomenon. Shout out your name, and you will hear it echoing back! The place also offers stunning views of the surrounding misty mountains. Kundala Lake: Situated around 20 kilometers from Munnar, Kundala Lake is a picturesque lake surrounded by lush green hills. You can enjoy boating on the lake and visit the nearby Kundala Tea Plantations. Top Station: The ultimate destination on this route is Top Station itself. It is situated around 32 kilometers from Munnar and is known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the Western Ghats. You can witness the stunning Neelakurinji flowers that bloom once every 12 years. Eravikulam National Park: Although not directly on the route from Munnar to Top Station.

Theni

Anayirangal Dam: As you continue your journey towards Theni, you will come across Anayirangal Dam. This scenic reservoir is surrounded by tea plantations and provides a picturesque setting for picnics and nature walks. Kolukkumalai Tea Estate: Situated near Munnar, Kolukkumalai is the highest tea estate in the world. The estate offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. You can take a guided tour to learn about the tea-making process and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea.

Chinnar

Marayoor Sandalwood Forests: Located on the route from Munnar to Chinnar, Marayoor is known for its sandalwood forests. Take a stroll through the aromatic sandalwood trees and explore the ancient rock paintings found in the region. Thoovanam Waterfalls: As you approach Chinnar, you'll find Thoovanam Waterfalls. Nestled in the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, these waterfalls offer a breathtaking sight amidst the lush greenery. The surrounding wildlife sanctuary is also home to various flora and fauna. Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary: Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary itself is a major tourist attraction. Spanning across the Western Ghats, this sanctuary is known for its diverse wildlife, including elephants, langurs, deer, and many bird species. You can take a safari or trek through the sanctuary to explore its natural beauty. Alampetty Viewpoint: Located near Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, Alampetty Viewpoint offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. It is a perfect spot to enjoy the scenic beauty of the region and capture some memorable photographs. Kanthalloor: Kanthalloor is a picturesque village known for its fruit orchards, vegetable farms, and beautiful landscapes. It is famous for its delicious varieties of apples, oranges, and other fruits. You can visit the farms, enjoy nature walks, and savor the local produce.
