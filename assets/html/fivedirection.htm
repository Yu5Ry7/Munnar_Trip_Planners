Top station

when traveling from Munnar to Top Station, there are several tourist spots along the way that you can visit. Here are some popular attractions: Mattupetty Dam: Located around 13 kilometers from Munnar, Mattupetty Dam offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and a beautiful reservoir. You can enjoy boating and also visit the nearby dairy farm. Echo Point: Around 15 kilometers from Munnar, Echo Point is known for its natural echo phenomenon. Shout out your name, and you will hear it echoing back! The place also offers stunning views of the surrounding misty mountains. Kundala Lake: Situated around 20 kilometers from Munnar, Kundala Lake is a picturesque lake surrounded by lush green hills. You can enjoy boating on the lake and visit the nearby Kundala Tea Plantations. Top Station: The ultimate destination on this route is Top Station itself. It is situated around 32 kilometers from Munnar and is known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the Western Ghats. You can witness the stunning Neelakurinji flowers that bloom once every 12 years. Eravikulam National Park: Although not directly on the route from Munnar to Top Station.

Cochin

AAthirapally Waterfalls: Located about 80 kilometers from Cochin, Athirapally Waterfalls is a magnificent waterfall nestled amidst lush green forests. It is one of the largest waterfalls in Kerala and offers a breathtaking sight. Valara Waterfalls: Situated near Adimali, Valara Waterfalls is another picturesque waterfall on the way to Cochin. The cascading waters surrounded by dense vegetation create a beautiful sight. Thattekad Bird Sanctuary: About 50 kilometers from Cochin, Thattekad Bird Sanctuary is a paradise for bird lovers. It is home to a wide variety of bird species, including rare and endemic ones. Exploring the sanctuary is a treat for nature enthusiasts. Kalady: Kalady is a small town near Cochin and holds great religious significance as the birthplace of Adi Shankara, the renowned Hindu philosopher. The town has temples dedicated to Adi Shankara and attracts pilgrims and spiritual seekers. Bolgatty Palace: Located on Bolgatty Island in Cochin, Bolgatty Palace is a beautiful heritage property surrounded by serene backwaters. It offers a glimpse into the colonial era and features stunning architecture and lush gardens. Fort Kochi: Fort Kochi is a historic neighborhood in Cochin known for its colonial charm. Explore the picturesque streets lined with colonial-era buildings, visit the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets, explore art galleries, and indulge in delicious seafood. Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace): This palace in Mattancherry showcases the Kerala architectural style and houses beautiful murals depicting Hindu mythology. It is a significant historical and cultural site in Cochin. Jewish Synagogue: Located in the heart of Mattancherry, the Jewish Synagogue is one of the oldest active synagogues in the Commonwealth countries. It is known for its exquisite interiors and antique artifacts. Marine Drive: A popular waterfront promenade in Cochin, Marine Drive offers stunning views of the backwaters and the city skyline. Take a leisurely walk, enjoy a boat ride, or simply relax and soak in the atmosphere. Hill Palace Museum: Situated in Tripunithura, near Cochin, the Hill Palace Museum is Kerala's largest archaeological museum. It houses a vast collection of artifacts, archaeological findings, and royal belongings.

Theni

AAnayirangal Dam: As you continue your journey towards Theni, you will come across Anayirangal Dam. This scenic reservoir is surrounded by tea plantations and provides a picturesque setting for picnics and nature walks. Kolukkumalai Tea Estate: Situated near Munnar, Kolukkumalai is the highest tea estate in the world. The estate offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. You can take a guided tour to learn about the tea-making process and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea. Suruli Falls: Located near Theni, Suruli Falls is a beautiful waterfall nestled amidst lush greenery. The falls cascade down from a height of approximately 150 feet and form a small pool at the bottom. It is a popular picnic spot and attracts visitors with its natural beauty. Meghamalai: Meghamalai, also known as the High Wavy Mountains, is a hill station near Theni. It is known for its tea estates, spice plantations, and scenic landscapes. The area is rich in flora and fauna, making it a paradise for nature lovers and trekking enthusiasts.

Chinnar

MMarayoor Sandalwood Forests: Located on the route from Munnar to Chinnar, Marayoor is known for its sandalwood forests. Take a stroll through the aromatic sandalwood trees and explore the ancient rock paintings found in the region. Thoovanam Waterfalls: As you approach Chinnar, you'll find Thoovanam Waterfalls. Nestled in the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, these waterfalls offer a breathtaking sight amidst the lush greenery. The surrounding wildlife sanctuary is also home to various flora and fauna. Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary: Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary itself is a major tourist attraction. Spanning across the Western Ghats, this sanctuary is known for its diverse wildlife, including elephants, langurs, deer, and many bird species. You can take a safari or trek through the sanctuary to explore its natural beauty. Alampetty Viewpoint: Located near Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, Alampetty Viewpoint offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. It is a perfect spot to enjoy the scenic beauty of the region and capture some memorable photographs. Kanthalloor: Kanthalloor is a picturesque village known for its fruit orchards, vegetable farms, and beautiful landscapes. It is famous for its delicious varieties of apples, oranges, and other fruits. You can visit the farms, enjoy nature walks, and savor the local produce.
